How I became a developer in eight months

Cristina Veale was a technical recruiter before she became a developer. That may not be the most common background for developers, but it gave her an uncommon edge in the job-seeking process. She knew which skills employers were looking for, how in-demand developers were (and still are), and that she didn't need a CS degree to have a programming career. "The harder it was for me to recruit people in IT, the more I realized how low the supply and high the demand was for programmers in the U.S." —Cristina Veale

Veale lists the free resources she used, the paid resources she considered, and the meetup groups she went to when she started learning to program.
